Rummy

DIFFICULTY: ▲▲▲     PLAYERS: 2+

Rummy is a classic card-matching game where 2 or more players aim to form sets and sequences by drawing and discarding cards. The goal is to be the first to arrange all your cards into valid combinations and declare your hand.

Common Terms

DRAW

When you take a card from the stock pile or discard pile.

DISCARD

When you place an unwanted card face-up on the discard pile.

DISCARD PILE

Face-up pile where all players discard cards.

A SEQUENCE

Three or more consecutive cards of the same suit (e.g., 5♥ 6♥ 7♥)

Pure Sequence - A sequence without any joker or wild card (required to declare in most variants)

A SET/SERIES/GROUP

Three or four cards of the same rank but different suits (e.g., 9♠ 9♥ 9♦).

Pure Set - A series that doesn't use a joker or wild card.

Impure Set - A series that uses a joker or wild card as a substitute.

Game Play

SETUP

Deal 7 cards to each player. Place remaining cards face-down as the stock pile. Flip one card face-up to declare a 'joker' and another to start the discard pile.

TAKING TURNS

On your turn (typically table moves clockwise), draw one card from either the stock pile or discard pile, then discard one card face-up. At the end of each round you should have no-more or no-less than 7 cards in hand.

FORMING COMBINATIONS

Arrange your cards into valid sequences and sets. You must have at least one pure sequence to declare.

DECLARING

When all your cards form valid combinations, discard your final card and declare. Other players reveal their hands and calculate points for unmatched cards.

WINNING

The player who declares first wins the round. Points are tallied based on unmatched cards in opponents' hands.
