Teen Patti

DIFFICULTY: ▲▲ NO.OF PLAYERS: 3-7

Teen Patti (meaning "three cards" in Hindi) is a popular Indian gambling card game similar to poker. Players compete to have the best three-card hand, with strategic betting and bluffing playing key roles in winning the pot!

Common Terms

Boot

The minimum stake that each player must put into the pot before cards are dealt. This is the entry amount for the round.

Pot

The total amount of money or chips in the center of the table. The winner of the round takes the entire pot.

Seen Player

A player who has looked at their cards. Seen players must bet double the current stake to continue playing.

Blind Player

A player who has not looked at their cards. Blind players can bet at the current stake rate and have strategic advantages.

Chaal

A bet made by a seen player. The chaal must be at least double the current stake but cannot exceed four times the stake.

Show

Requesting to compare cards with another player. This can only happen when two players remain, ending the round.

Sideshow

A seen player can request to privately compare cards with the previous player (if that player is also seen). The previous player can accept or decline.

Pack/Fold

Dropping out of the current round. The player surrenders their claim to the pot and loses any money already bet.

GAME PLAY

Setup

Players sit in a circle and agree on the boot amount (minimum stake). Each player contributes the boot to the pot. A standard 52-card deck is used, with Aces ranking highest and 2s ranking lowest.

The Deal

The dealer distributes three cards face-down to each player, one at a time in clockwise order. Players can choose to look at their cards (becoming "seen") or play without looking (staying "blind").

Betting Rounds

Starting with the player to the dealer's left, players take turns betting. Blind players bet the current stake, while seen players must bet double. Players can bet, raise (up to 4x current stake), call for a sideshow (if applicable), request a show (when only two players remain), or fold. Betting continues until only one player remains or two players agree to a show.

Sideshow Rules

A seen player can request a sideshow with the previous player if that player is also seen. The previous player can accept or decline. If accepted, both players privately compare hands, and the player with the weaker hand must fold. If the hands are equal, the player who requested the sideshow must fold.

The Showdown

When only two players remain, either can pay for a show (blind players pay the current stake, seen players pay double). Both players reveal their cards, and the player with the better hand wins the pot. If both hands are equal, the player who did not pay for the show wins.

HAND RANKINGS

Hands are ranked from highest to lowest:

  • Trail (Three of a Kind) - Three cards of the same rank (e.g., A♠ A♥ A♦). A-A-A is the best trail.
  • Pure Sequence (Straight Flush) - Three consecutive cards of the same suit (e.g., K♥ Q♥ J♥). A-K-Q is the best sequence.
  • Sequence (Straight) - Three consecutive cards not all in the same suit (e.g., K♥ Q♠ J♦).
  • Color (Flush) - Three cards of the same suit that are not in sequence (e.g., A♠ K♠ 9♠).
  • Pair - Two cards of the same rank (e.g., K♥ K♠ 5♦). A-A-K is the best pair.
  • High Card - None of the above. The hand with the highest card wins (e.g., A♥ J♠ 9♦).

Comparing Hands

When hands are of the same type, compare the highest card. For trails and pairs, compare the rank of the matching cards first. For sequences, A-K-Q is highest, then K-Q-J, down to 4-3-2, with A-2-3 being the lowest sequence.

Winning Strategies

Playing blind can be advantageous as you bet half the amount of seen players. Bluffing is crucial—you can win with weak hands if opponents fold. Watch betting patterns and know when to fold to minimize losses. Sideshow requests can help eliminate opponents before the final showdown.

Variations

Many regional variations exist, including Muflis (lowest hand wins), AK47 (A, K, 4, and 7 are wild cards), and Best of Four (deal four cards, play with best three). House rules should be agreed upon before starting.
