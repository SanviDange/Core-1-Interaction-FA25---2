Poker

DIFFICULTY: ▲▲▲ NO.OF PLAYERS: 2-10

Poker is a classic betting and strategy card game where players compete to make the best five-card hand or bluff their opponents into folding. Combine skill, psychology, and a bit of luck to win the pot!

Common Terms

Basic Play

Ante

A small forced bet that all players must place before the hand begins to seed the pot.

Blinds

Forced bets placed by the two players to the left of the dealer (small blind and big blind) before cards are dealt.

Fold

When you discard your hand and forfeit any chance of winning the current pot.

Call

Matching the current bet to stay in the hand.

Raise

Increasing the current bet, forcing other players to match the new amount to stay in.

Check

Passing the action to the next player without betting, only possible when no bet has been made.

Increased Difficulty

Bluff

Betting or raising with a weak hand to convince opponents you have a strong hand and make them fold.

All-In

Betting all of your remaining chips. You can only win the portion of the pot that matches your bet.

Showdown

When betting rounds are complete and remaining players reveal their hands to determine the winner.

The Flop

In Texas Hold'em, the first three community cards dealt face-up on the table.

The Turn

In Texas Hold'em, the fourth community card dealt after the flop.

The River

In Texas Hold'em, the fifth and final community card dealt.

GAME PLAY (Texas Hold'em)

Setup

Players sit around a table with a designated dealer position (marked by a dealer button). The two players to the left of the dealer post the small blind and big blind. Each player receives two private cards (hole cards) dealt face-down.

Pre-Flop Betting

Starting with the player to the left of the big blind, each player decides to fold, call the big blind, or raise. Betting continues clockwise until all active players have matched the highest bet.

The Flop

Three community cards are dealt face-up in the center of the table. A new betting round begins with the first active player to the left of the dealer. Players can check, bet, call, raise, or fold.

The Turn

A fourth community card is dealt face-up. Another betting round occurs with the same options as the flop.

The River

The fifth and final community card is dealt face-up. The final betting round takes place.

Showdown

If more than one player remains after the final betting round, players reveal their hole cards. The player with the best five-card hand (using any combination of their two hole cards and the five community cards) wins the pot.

Winning

Win by having the best hand at showdown or by being the last player remaining after all others have folded. The pot goes to the winner, and a new hand begins with the dealer button moving one position clockwise.

HAND RANKINGS

Breaking Ties

If two players have the same hand ranking, the player with the highest cards in that hand wins. For example, a pair of Aces beats a pair of Kings. If both hands are identical, the pot is split equally.

Using Community Cards

You can use both of your hole cards, one hole card, or none of your hole cards (playing the board) to make your best five-card hand. The best possible combination wins.
