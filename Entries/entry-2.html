GAME PLAY

Setup

Deal 7 cards to each player. Place remaining cards face-down as the stock pile. Flip one card face-up to declare a 'joker' and another to start the discard pile.

Card Values

Number cards are worth their face value, face cards (J, Q, K) are worth 10, and Aces are worth 1 or 11.

Player Turns

Starting from the dealer's left, each player decides to hit, stand, double down, or split. Continue hitting until you stand or bust.

Dealer's Turn

After all players finish, the dealer reveals their face-down card. The dealer must hit on 16 or less and stand on 17 or more.

Winning

Beat the dealer by having a higher hand value without busting. Blackjack pays 3:2, regular wins pay 1:1. If you and the dealer tie, it's a push (bet returned).

Card counting is a strategy used to track the ratio of high to low cards remaining in the deck. While not illegal, casinos frown upon it and may ask you to leave if caught.


The Hi-Lo System

  • Low cards (2-6) = +1
  • Neutral cards (7-9) = 0
  • High cards (10-Ace) = -1
  • Positive count = More high cards remain (player advantage)
  • Negative count = More low cards remain (dealer advantage)

Running Count vs True Count

The running count is your ongoing tally as cards are dealt. In multi-deck games, calculate the true count by dividing the running count by the estimated number of decks remaining. Example: Running count +6 √∑ 3 decks remaining = True count +2.

Betting Strategy

Adjust your bets based on the true count. When the count is positive (+2 or higher), increase your bet size. When the count is negative or neutral, bet the minimum. Vary bet sizes gradually to avoid detection.

Playing Adjustments

  • High positive count: Hit on 12 vs dealer's 2 or 3, double down more often, take insurance
  • Negative count: Play conservatively, stick to basic strategy
  • Always combine counting with basic strategy for best results
