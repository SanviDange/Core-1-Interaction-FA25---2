Black Jack

DIFFICULTY: ▲▲▲ NO.OF PLAYERS: Dealer + 1+

Blackjack is a fast-paced casino card game where players compete against the dealer to get a hand value as close to 21 as possible without going over. You beat the dealer's hand to win!

Common Terms

Basic Play

Hit

When it’s your turn, ‘hitting’ means requesting for another card.

Bust

When the sum of your cards exceeds 21, you ‘bust’ i,e. you lose the round.

Stand

When you ‘stand’ or keep the hand you are dealt with and choose not to ‘hit’.

Push

When both, you and the dealer end the rounds with the same hand value, it's a tie. Your bet is ‘pushed’ or returned.

Increased Difficulty

Insurance

If the dealer’s up-facing card is an ace, players will be given the ‘insurance’ option. If the dealer gets blackjack (ace + ten), then players who took insurance will receive a payout.

Split

If you’re given two of the same cards as your starting hand, you can separate the pair and play two hands. You’ll need to double your wager.

Doubling

After your second card is revealed, you’ll be given the option to ‘Double.’ This will double your wager, and you’ll be assigned one more card. After that card is assigned, you won’t have the option to hit.

GAME PLAY

Setup

Deal 7 cards to each player. Place remaining cards face-down as the stock pile. Flip one card face-up to declare a 'joker' and another to start the discard pile.

Card Values

Number cards are worth their face value, face cards (J, Q, K) are worth 10, and Aces are worth 1 or 11.

Player Turns

Starting from the dealer's left, each player decides to hit, stand, double down, or split. Continue hitting until you stand or bust.

Dealer's Turn

After all players finish, the dealer reveals their face-down card. The dealer must hit on 16 or less and stand on 17 or more.

Winning

Beat the dealer by having a higher hand value without busting. Blackjack pays 3:2, regular wins pay 1:1. If you and the dealer tie, it's a push (bet returned).
