Common Terms

Basic Play

Hit

When it’s your turn, ‘hitting’ means requesting for another card.

Bust

When the sum of your cards exceeds 21, you ‘bust’ i,e. you lose the round.

Stand

When you ‘stand’ or keep the hand you are dealt with and choose not to ‘hit’.

Push

When both, you and the dealer end the rounds with the same hand value, it's a tie. Your bet is ‘pushed’ or returned.

Increased Difficulty

Insurance

If the dealer’s up-facing card is an ace, players will be given the ‘insurance’ option. If the dealer gets blackjack (ace + ten), then players who took insurance will receive a payout.

Tertiary color example text

Split

If you’re given two of the same cards as your starting hand, you can separate the pair and play two hands. You’ll need to double your wager.

Highlight color example text

Doubling

After your second card is revealed, you’ll be given the option to ‘Double.’ This will double your wager, and you’ll be assigned one more card. After that card is assigned, you won’t have the option to hit.
