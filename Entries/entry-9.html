Bridge is a strategic trick-taking card game played by four players in two competing partnerships. Partners sit opposite each other and work together through bidding and card play to win tricks and fulfill their contract!

Common Terms

Basic Play

Trick

A round of four cards, one played by each player. The highest card in the led suit (or the highest trump) wins the trick.

Trump

The suit declared in the final contract that ranks higher than all other suits. A trump card beats any card from other suits.

Lead

Playing the first card of a trick. Other players must follow suit if possible.

Dummy

The partner of the declarer. After the opening lead, dummy places their cards face-up on the table for the declarer to play.

Declarer

The player who first bid the suit (or no trump) of the final contract. This player controls both their own hand and dummy's hand.

Increased Difficulty

Bid

A statement during the auction indicating how many tricks above six your partnership will take and which suit will be trump (or no trump).

Contract

The final bid that was not followed by three passes. It determines how many tricks the declaring partnership must win to succeed.

No Trump

A contract played without a trump suit. The highest card in the suit led always wins the trick.

Overtricks

Tricks won by the declarer beyond those required by the contract. These earn bonus points.

Undertricks

When the declarer fails to make their contract, each trick short of the contract counts as an undertrick and gives points to the opponents.

Setup

Four players sit in compass positions (North-South vs East-West). Deal all 52 cards clockwise, giving each player 13 cards. Partners sit across from each other.

The Auction

Starting with the dealer, players bid in clockwise order. Each bid must be higher than the previous bid (either more tricks or a higher-ranking suit). Players can also pass, double, or redouble. The auction ends when three consecutive players pass.

The Play

The player to the left of the declarer makes the opening lead. Dummy then lays their cards face-up, organized by suit. The declarer plays cards from both their hand and dummy's hand. Players must follow suit if possible; if not, they may play any card. The highest card in the led suit (or highest trump) wins the trick. The winner of each trick leads the next.

Making the Contract

The declaring partnership must win at least the number of tricks specified in their contract. Remember that bids are stated as tricks above six (a bid of "3 Hearts" means 9 total tricks).

Winning

If the declarer makes their contract, their partnership scores points. If they fail, the defending partnership scores points based on the number of undertricks. The first partnership to score 100 points "below the line" wins a game. Winning two games wins the rubber and awards bonus points.

Contract Points

Points for tricks bid and made are scored "below the line" and count toward winning a game. Only these points count toward the 100 needed for game.

Bonus Points

Overtricks, slam bonuses, and doubled/redoubled contracts earn bonus points scored "above the line." Making a doubled contract also awards a 50-point bonus. Winning the rubber earns 500 points (for winning 2-0) or 300 points (for winning 2-1).

Penalty Points

If the contract fails, the defending side scores 50 points per undertrick when not vulnerable, or 100 points per undertrick when vulnerable. These penalties double if the contract was doubled, and double again if redoubled.
