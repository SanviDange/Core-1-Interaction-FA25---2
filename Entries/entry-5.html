Go Fish

DIFFICULTY: â–² PLAYERS: 2-6

Go Fish is a simple card game where players collect sets of four matching cards by asking opponents for specific ranks. The player who collects the most complete sets wins!

Common Terms

Go Fish

What a player says when they don't have the requested card, telling the asker to draw from the stock pile.

Book/Set

Four cards of the same rank (e.g., four 7s). When completed, place it face-up in front of you.

Stock Pile

The face-down pile of remaining cards that players draw from.

Game Play

Setup:

Deal 7 cards to each player (5 cards if playing with 4+ players). Place remaining cards face-down as the stock pile. Players look at their cards and keep them hidden.

Taking Turns:

On your turn, ask any opponent for cards of a specific rank that you already hold (e.g., "Do you have any 5s?"). You must have at least one card of that rank in your hand to ask for it.

Receiving Cards:

If the opponent has cards of that rank, they must give you all of them. You can continue asking any player for cards as long as you receive matches.

Go Fish:

If the opponent doesn't have the requested rank, they say "Go Fish!" Draw one card from the stock pile. If you draw the rank you asked for, reveal it and take another turn. Otherwise, your turn ends.

Making books:

When you collect all four cards of the same rank, immediately place the book face-up in front of you and remove it from your hand.

Winning: -

The game ends when all books are completed or the stock pile runs out. The player with the most books wins!
