Crazy Eights

DIFFICULTY: ▲ NO.OF PLAYERS: 2-7

Crazy Eights is a classic shedding-type card game where players race to get rid of all their cards by matching either the rank or suit of the top card on the discard pile. Eights are wild and can change the suit at any time!

Common Terms

Match

Playing a card that matches either the rank or suit of the top card on the discard pile.

Draw

When you can't play a card, you must draw one card from the stock pile. If you can play it immediately, you may do so.

Wild Card

An eight can be played on any card and allows the player to declare a new suit for the next play.

Knock/Pass

After drawing, if you still can't play, you knock or say "pass" to end your turn.

Going Out

When a player plays their last card, they "go out" and win the round.

GAME PLAY

Setup

Deal 5 cards to each player (7 cards if playing with 2 players). Place the remaining cards face-down as the stock pile. Flip the top card face-up to start the discard pile.

Objective

Be the first player to get rid of all your cards. You can play a card if it matches the rank or suit of the top card on the discard pile, or if it's an eight.

Player Turns

On your turn, play one card that matches the rank or suit of the top discard card. If you play an eight, declare the new suit. If you can't play, draw one card from the stock pile. If playable, you may play it immediately; otherwise, your turn ends.

Special Rules

Eights are wild and can be played at any time. When you play an eight, announce which suit the next player must follow. If the stock pile runs out, shuffle the discard pile (except the top card) to create a new stock pile.

Winning

The first player to play all their cards wins the round.

SCORING
  • Eights = 50 points
  • Face cards (J, Q, K) = 10 points
  • Aces = 1 point
  • Number cards = Face value

Calculating Score

The winner of each round scores zero points. All other players add up the point values of their remaining cards and this becomes their score for the round.

Game End

Play multiple rounds, keeping a cumulative score. The game typically ends when one player reaches a predetermined score (commonly 100 or 200 points). The player with the lowest total score at that point wins the game.
