Solitaire is a single-player card game where you organize a shuffled deck by suit and rank. The goal is to move all cards to four foundation piles, building them up from Ace to King in each suit.

Common Terms

Draw

Take cards from the stock pile.

Build Down

Place cards on the tableau in descending rank order, alternating colors.

Build Up

Place cards on foundations in ascending order by suit.

Move

Transfer a card or sequence between tableau columns.

Game Play

Setup:

Deal seven tableau columns with the first column having one card, the second having two cards, and so on. Only the top card in each column is face-up. Place remaining cards face-down as the stock pile.

Building the Tableau: -

Move face-up cards between columns in descending order, alternating red and black suits (e.g., red 8 on black 9). You can move sequences of cards together if they follow this pattern.

Using Foundations:

When an Ace appears, move it to a foundation pile. Build up each foundation by suit in ascending order (A, 2, 3... K).

Drawing Cards:

When no moves are available on the tableau, draw cards from the stock pile one or three at a time (depending on variant).
